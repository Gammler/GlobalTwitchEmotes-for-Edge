Website Filters

Disable (or exclusively enable) Global Twitch Emotes on certain websites.
Asterisk wildcards supported.

Examples:

"youtube.com" will trigger ONLY on the homepage of YouTube, while

"youtube.com/*" will trigger on ALL addresses starting with youtube.com, such as the following:

  • http://www.youtube.com
  • https://www.youtube.com/feed/subscriptions
  • https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qkUVToIfrKg

Global Twitch Emotes

    By Mohamed El-Alawi

Hi there! My name's Mohamed El-Alawi and I'm currently an undergraduate student majoring in computer science. I've been passionate about all things programming for my entire life, and while I've started and finished countless little personal projects, most were just that: personal, private, and unshared. I loved to tinker with little bits of code on my own, but I had yet to actually release anything. I won't lie, the thought of doing so did scare me at first.

Eventually, I realized that working solely in clandestine was inhibiting my own growth as a programmer, and the only way I could truly improve my skills was to throw my creations out there, to be judged by users such as yourself. I've been a huge fan of Twitch.tv ever since its debut in 2011, and I figured that my first 'official' software release should target the community I love. So here it is, Global Twitch Emotes. I sincerely hope you enjoy it.
